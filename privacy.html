ListingLens — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

ListingLens is a Chrome extension built by Clingogo that helps Amazon sellers analyze their product listings and customer reviews. This page explains what data the extension accesses, where it goes, and what it does not do.

What data ListingLens accesses

When you use ListingLens on an Amazon product or review page, the extension reads:

  • Publicly visible listing content (title, bullet points, description, price, rating)
  • Publicly visible customer reviews on the page you have open
  • Review text you manually paste into the extension

ListingLens does not access your Amazon account, login session, order history, payment information, or Seller Central data. It only reads what is already rendered on the page in your browser.

Where your data is stored

All extracted listing data, reviews, and generated reports are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's storage.local API. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to ListingLens or Clingogo servers.

AI analysis (OpenAI / Gemini)

When you click "Analyze," ListingLens sends the extracted listing and review data directly from your browser to the AI provider you have selected in Settings (OpenAI or Google Gemini), using an API key that you provide yourself. This data goes directly to OpenAI or Google — it does not pass through any ListingLens server.

Your API key is stored locally in your browser and is never transmitted to ListingLens, Clingogo, or any third party other than the AI provider itself.

Please refer to OpenAI's Privacy Policy or Google's Privacy Policy for how each provider handles data sent through their API.

License and subscription data

If you activate a ListingLens Pro license, your license key is sent to our license verification server to confirm your subscription status. We store the minimum data necessary to validate your plan (license key, plan type, usage count) and do not store your Amazon listing or review content on any server.

What ListingLens does not do

  • Does not collect or store your Amazon login credentials
  • Does not access Seller Central or Amazon Ads accounts
  • Does not sell or share your data with advertisers
  • Does not track your browsing activity outside of Amazon product/review pages
  • Does not bypass Amazon's review display limits, automate purchases, automate login, or perform actions in Seller Central

Permissions explained

  • storage — to save user settings, selected AI provider, locally stored API keys, captured listing/review data, and generated reports in your browser
  • activeTab / scripting — to read listing and visible review content from the Amazon tab you're viewing, only when you open the extension or click an extraction action
  • Amazon host permissions — to run on specific Amazon product and review page domains. ListingLens does not use a broad all_urls permission
  • OpenAI / Google Gemini host permissions — to send selected listing/review content directly from your browser to the AI provider you selected, using your own API key
  • tabs (optional) — only requested at runtime if you explicitly enable Advanced Auto Collect, so ListingLens can monitor or update the active Amazon review tab during that optional workflow
  • alarms — used only by the optional Advanced Auto Collect feature to schedule delayed, best-effort review capture steps after explicit user opt-in

Your choices

You can clear all stored data at any time from the extension's Reviews tab ("Clear Reviews") or by removing the extension from Chrome, which deletes all local storage associated with it.
